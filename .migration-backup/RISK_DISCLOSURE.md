## 2. MARKET RISK

### 2.1 High Volatility

Market prices fluctuate constantly and can move dramatically:

- **Cryptocurrency**: Moves of 10-50%+ in a single day are common
- **Forex**: Currency pairs can swing 1-3% intraday
- **Commodities**: Subject to supply shocks and geopolitical events
- **Stocks**: Individual companies can fall 50%+ in minutes (e.g., bankruptcy news)

**You can lose substantial capital quickly in volatile markets.**

### 2.2 Gap Risk

Markets can "gap"—jump suddenly between prices—when:
- Major news announcements occur
- Financial data is released
- Trading halts due to system issues
- Markets open after major events

**If you have a stop-loss order, it may execute far worse than your intended price during a gap.**

### 2.3 Liquidity Risk

- Some assets trade with low volume, making orders difficult to fill
- Your order may execute at a significantly worse price than displayed
- You may be unable to close a position at a desired price
- "Slippage" (difference between expected and actual price) can be substantial

## 3. LEVERAGE RISK

### 3.1 What is Leverage?

Leverage allows you to control larger positions with a smaller deposit:

- **1:2 Leverage**: You control $2 with $1 of capital
- **1:50 Leverage**: You control $50 with $1 of capital
- **1:100 Leverage**: You control $100 with $1 of capital

### 3.2 How Leverage Amplifies Losses

**Example: $1,000 deposit with 1:50 leverage**

- You can control $50,000 worth of position
- If the market moves 2% against you: You lose $1,000 (your entire deposit)
- If the market moves 5% against you: You lose $2,500 (more than your deposit)

**You can lose more than you deposited.**

### 3.3 Leverage Liquidation

When your losses reach a certain level ("liquidation price"):
- Your position is forcibly closed by the platform
- You lock in losses at the worst possible time
- Liquidation may occur at far worse prices than your stop-loss order
- You may still owe money if liquidation doesn't fully recover the loss

### 4.1 Regulatory Risk

- Governments may ban or heavily restrict crypto trading
- Regulations vary dramatically by country
- Your jurisdiction may ban crypto or require special licenses
- Regulatory changes can cause 30-50%+ price swings

### 4.2 Technical Risk

- **Wallet Hacking**: Your connected wallet could be compromised
- **Exchange Hacking**: Crypto exchanges are regularly hacked (Mt. Gox, FTX collapse)
- **Smart Contract Bugs**: Code errors can cause fund loss
- **Blockchain Forks**: Network splits can affect holdings
- **51% Attacks**: Attackers controlling majority hash power can reverse transactions

### 4.3 Counterparty Risk

When you trade crypto on our platform:
- Your funds are in our custody (we control the private keys)
- If we go bankrupt, you may lose your funds
- Our insurance may not cover all losses
- Regulatory protection varies by jurisdiction

### 4.4 Crypto Project Risk

Many cryptocurrencies are:
- **Highly Speculative**: Unproven technology
- **Scams**: Exit scams where founders steal investor money
- **Over-Hyped**: Price driven by hype, not fundamentals
- **Low Adoption**: Few real-world uses
- **Developer Dependent**: Failure to develop can kill a project

### 4.5 No Intrinsic Value

Cryptocurrency value depends entirely on:
- Future adoption
- Market sentiment
- Regulatory developments
- Competing technologies

**There is no fundamental valuation model.** Prices can fall to zero.

## 12. PSYCHOLOGICAL RISKS

### 12.1 Overconfidence

- Early wins lead to excessive risk-taking
- Traders often overestimate their skill
- Overconfidence increases position sizes until major loss occurs
- This creates "blow-up" scenarios

### 12.2 Loss Aversion

- Reluctance to accept losses leads to "holding losers"
- Hoping markets will reverse is not a strategy
- Losses can compound if positions aren't closed
- Paralysis prevents risk management

### 12.3 Revenge Trading

- After a loss, traders attempt larger trades to "get even"
- This emotional trading usually increases losses
- Discipline is hard to maintain after large losses
